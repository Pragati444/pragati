My Hobbies Gallery

A collection of my favorite activities and interests

Everyone has unique hobbies that make life more enjoyable. Below you'll find some of my favorite pastimes, each with its own special appeal. Click on any hobby to learn more about how I engage with it.

Photography

Creative Outdoors

Capturing moments and telling stories through the lens. From landscapes to street photography, I love exploring different perspectives.

My photography journey began 5 years ago when I received my first DSLR. I specialize in landscape and portrait photography, using both digital and film cameras. I particularly enjoy night photography and long exposure techniques.

Gaming

Strategy Tech

Immersive worlds, challenging puzzles, and competitive gameplay. A great way to unwind and connect with friends.

I enjoy a mix of competitive multiplayer games and story-driven single player experiences. Currently into RTS games and RPGs with rich lore. I also occasionally stream gameplay when I have time.

Hiking

Adventure Fitness

Exploring nature's beauty one trail at a time. Fresh air, exercise, and breathtaking views make for perfect weekends.

I've completed over 200 miles of trails in the past 3 years, including several multi-day backpacking trips. My favorite hikes include alpine lake destinations and desert canyon treks. Always carrying my camera to document the journey!

Reading

Knowledge Relaxation

Getting lost in different worlds and expanding my perspectives. Mostly fiction, with some non-fiction mixed in.

I typically read 30-40 books per year across various genres. My favorite authors include Haruki Murakami and Octavia Butler. Recently started a book club with friends focusing on science fiction and fantasy works from diverse authors.

Woodworking

Handcraft Creative

Creating functional art with my hands. There's something magical about turning raw lumber into beautiful objects.

I practice both hand tool and power tool woodworking, with a focus on furniture and decorative items. Recently built a custom walnut desk with hand-cut dovetail joints. My next project is a small bookcase with Japanese joinery techniques.
